Description

As a people of Hakka, I have always wanted to create works that reflect my background and the rich diverse pattern of my culture. The red and green Hakka flower cloth and the big peony flowers represent wealth and joy; in recent years, they have become the representative of Hakka culture.

However, the people who make traditional Hakka clothing industry, believe that Hakka flower cloth actually originated from Japan. During the Japanese colonialization in Taiwan, the flower cloth became popular. Most of the cultures rejected to continue using this pattern by the end of colonialization, except the Hakka people. So afterward, the flower cloth was seen as a symbol of Hakka, and the flower cloth was regarded as a representative of Hakka's colonialized history.

Design Process

I started out illustrating the patterns in Adobe illustrator, there is actually a specific technic used to draw the flowers to have it being considered as a Hakka Pattern. Which makes my work easier since I only have to simply reference it.



I later export them into svg and import them using the same svg to p5 tool that I used in the previous assignment. I made 5 different kinds of leaves and 4 different kinds of flowers, and used random function for determining their positions, rotated degrees and scales.

Reflection

Though it's less of an "optical illusion" work that was mentioned in the assignment requirment. I am actually really impressed with my final outcome! This is the first time I have incorporate my own culture into my work using codes, and I really think the outcome looks fresh and new. I also really enjoyed reading and researching history about my own culture, and was surprised that I have never learned about the colonialized history of the pattern before.

The experience was both visually eye-opening and also mentally to me.

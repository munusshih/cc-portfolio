#3 Experimental Clock


*clicke on it*

Description

Despite looking like a normal clock at first glance, it actually expands into a multi-dimensional clock.

I came from Taiwan where it has 12 hours difference from New York, so if you compare the clock used in Taiwan with the clock used in New York, they are identical. And it sort of gives you that eery feeling that the time is synchronous. Yet it is not, and with that 12 hours difference, it's hard to video chat with my friends and family in taiwan, because when it's daytime here, it's midnight there. I wanted to address this strange feeling of synchronous and unsynchronous both at once using an expandable clock.

Design Process

At first glance, you might feel like, oh it's a simple clock. Like when you see how identical clocks in Taiwan are to the clocks in New York. But when you click on it, it expands into this complex, multi-layers of clocks that grants you knowledge in what they might be doing right now and what you're supposed to do.

It also guides you through the possible time of video chatting with your friends and family. It is definitely hard to read, and it's indeed inhuman and unintuitive, but that's exactly what I feel like when I try to understand the 12 hr difference we have. If you want to, you could always just release the mouse and synchronize it again back to its simple form.

Before making this clock, I actually made another clock that only uses particles that rotate and create different shapes throughout the day. That kind of inspires me to make something as complex but functional, and address the topic in a more personal way. Making my own clock. After all, time is subjective, and it takes time to learn how to read this clock, just as all the clocks.

Reflection

Time is a difficult thing to design for. Not only because that it is ever-changing, but also because that it is so relatable that everyone has their own personal experience to it. Through the process of making this clock, I realized that although we often view time as objective, it is fairly personal and private.

Whether it is social, cultural or political, we all cannot escape from being with time, contexualizing with time and interpret time by ourselves. The most challenging part of making this piece might be that - since there are already so many great works about time that exist, what else could have been done?

Description

I personally love to draw faces, and this is my first attempt to transform my drawing into a random generator. This piece of work is B&W because I wanted the viewers to notice that squiggly, hand-drawn style in my lines using random functions.

Not only generating the faces using the random function but also their names are generated randomly from a list of thousand unisex names on the internet. Every one of them generated from the random function has a unique name and is part of the new school, thus the font I used here is our customized new school font.

They would introduce themselves every time you create them.

Design Process

I love to sketch, but I didn't spend much time on drafting this time. I was trying to implement the idea of "coding as painting", and just get my hands on the coding process without thinking too much. Change the concept as I'm coding new things, get into 'the zone'.

I first started with the face, and gradually add more features to it. Before finishing the whole thing, I feel like there's something missing. What else do we usually think of when we think of someone's face? What is something that makes this face unique among all the faces we've seen. Then, ding dong! The idea of generating their names randomly popped into my head, and I soon found this library called 'p5.speech' to take my idea to the next level.

Not only did I successfully pulled their names from a list of a thousand unisex names, but I also use p5.speech to randomly assign them their speech pitch, speech rate, and greeting words.

sayer.setLang("es-US");
sayer.setPitch(random(0.01, 2));
sayer.setRate(random(1.2, 1.5));

Reflection

Some people would claim that drawing on paper with pen versus drawing with code on screen is fundamentally the same if you excludes the difference in medium. Yet I found out that the whole process of drawing, the thinking behind it as well as the possibilities are nothing alike to be honest. Here I quote the words professor Justin Bakse said about why he loves coding, "these things are alive".

I also reflect heavily through the process of image-making on what xinxin said in the lecture video, "randomness is embeded in our culture". The concept behind a face generator is to parametized faces, extract features from them, flatten them and mapping into another face. If the process of drawing faces is a humanistic approach to our ideas of faces, then the process of coding one is a mechanic - even A.I. oriented - process of generating.

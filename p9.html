Description

It is a consentful interface built on top of my experimental camera.

Design Process

The idea is to use "F.R.I.E.S." model to build a consentful interface for my experimental camera.


The three things I can think of that needs the consent is "the access to camera", "the access to mouseX, mouseY" and the consent to even use this sketch. I designed these three consents in different format. I will elaborate how I use the FRIES model to design the interface below.


Freely Given


In order to make the user feels like they are not being forced to consent but rather it's an option for them. I also design the front animated page of the interface. So users who do not want their camera to be accessed can also look at the animated front page without being bored.


Reversible, Enthusiastic, Informed


Users can check the box themselves to consent to the access of their camera or mouse data. They can always uncheck the box to reverse the action when they are no longer enthusiastic.


Specific


The two checkbox has vey distinct instruction. Instead of saying "Consent to access the mouse", I used a more specific language "Consent to Access the XY Position of Mouse" to inform the user.

Reflection

I love the idea of consentful interface. I think it is very powerful and is something that seperate us from "engineers" who would simply code the interface out.
I definitely want to dive more into the idea of that and learn how designers can build better interface using these humanistic knowledges.
